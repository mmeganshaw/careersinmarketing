Insights Management

A Day in the Life

There are a variety of different types of Insights roles, but one activity they all have in common is the daily use of data to tell a story and the translation of information to inform decisions.

A typical entry-level position is largely focused on syndicated data, where daily activities include demonstrating expertise and competency using various data analysis tools to find actionable insights and to answer questions about consumers, brands, products, and trends.

Another type of Insights role involves proprietary, primary research which means being involved in the entire data process, from collection to analysis. Someone in this type of role might start their day by working on some research for a new product, which might start with idea generation of the type of data needed, white space exploration, and even testing new concepts with consumers. Once the product has evolved, commercialization research is done to understand the impact of any proposed communication materials or brand assets. You might set up a mocked up store environment for consumers to shop, watch a panel of consumers react to packaging, or collect data from in-home usage studies, then synthesize the data into a reporting summary that can be used to inform decisions about the brand or the product.
