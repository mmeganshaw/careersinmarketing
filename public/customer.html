Customer Marketing

A Day in the Life

The week starts by meeting with the Client Strategy/Marketing Team. If you are on the B2B side of things, you are meeting to talk through what programs you are currently running for certain clients, the status of each program, and what the needs are from the client. If you are on the B2C side of things, you are reviewing the current programs that you are running with vendors, understanding some preliminary results from current campaigns and looking to understand/ figure how to optimize campaigns. As the day begins, you are working with your Marketing team to create and implement strategies to acquire new customers while also focusing on retaining current customers.

Acquisition: you will work to create strategies with Vendors, internal teams and production teams to implement programs surrounding the Digital, Email & Direct mail verticals. If your company has the ability to work in a cross-functional environment, you will be able to work hand in hand with the media teams to implement potential acquisition strategies in the TV & Radio verticals.

(looking at Digital, Email & Direct Mail) After you have identified the strategies, you need to know who to market too. So you will work with vendors (preferably taking a data driven approach) to understand who your best customers are and targeting those similar.

On a weekly basis you will be in communication with those vendors to help optimize current programs which are hopefully yielding good results.

Retention: Only a daily/ weekly basis you will look to understand your customer retention rate. With increasing competition, it is important to have just as great of a Defense as Offense. You will work with the marketing team to implement strategies like understanding churn rates, or unsubscribe rates and figuring out what is causing that (i.e. lack of customer service, unique offering etc.)
