Customer Experience

A Day in the Life

Each day you walk into work and happily greet your customer experience team. There are a group of 3-4 analysts that report to you as the Customer Experience Manager. The size of your firm will generally determine the overall size of your team.

You begin your day by checking your emails to make sure there aren’t any customer emergencies that you need to tackle right away. Today you make it through your inbox with no issues in sight. As soon as you finish going through your inbox, one of your analysts sends in his report regarding the new mobile application that your firm released a month ago. You had been collecting customer feedback since the launch, and the VP of Operations has been hungry for your team's insights for weeks. You begin going over each analysts report, looking for commonalities to fill your whitepaper that will be sent to the VP.

Just as you’re putting the final touches on your whitepaper, the Director of Marketing calls you, frantically asking for help. A social media post regarding one of your lower level workers ranting negatively about the firm has gone viral. How are customers reacting? Is this post even relevant to them? Will this PR nightmare affect our goals for the quarter? You bring in your team and ask them to begin crafting a survey that will be used to gather insights from customers, as well as a plan to get some of the more active social media influencers in your market on the phone to gather their feedback.

By now it’s close to the end of the workday, and you submit your whitepaper to the VP of Ops just in time.

Other Relevant Duties:
- Test products and messaging with customers prior to distribution
- Track progress of products in the marketplace
- First and last responsible person for customer feedback
- Optimizing the product lifecycle
