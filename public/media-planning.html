Media Planning & Buying

A Day in the Life

As a media planner, you start your day by walking into your agency and greeting the rest of your team. You grab some coffee and begin checking your emails. You’ve begun working on a new venture for a client who is releasing a new hair product mid-summer. They’ve hired your firm to handle the promotion of the launch.

The first meeting of the day is with the rest of the account team, where you all pour over the brief and strategize what message you're going to push, who you’re targeting, and (where you come in), what mediums the media will run on and for how long. Once the meeting is over, you begin making phone calls to all relevant outlets so you can start getting quotes for billboards, physical placements, radio, and TV spots. By knowing what’s currently available and what will be available, you can strategize what will make the most sense for your client's project and their budget.

Once you have quotes for those outlets, you start divvying up the budget in a way you think makes sense. You’ll allocate 10% to radio, 40% to social media, 30% to TV, 10% to billboard, and 10% to paid search. You submit your media plan with associated costs to your account manager, just in time for the end of the workday.

Other Relevant Duties:
- Producing media plans and forecasts
- Undertaking relevant research
- Analyzing data
- Liaising with clients, consumers and advertising staff Producing briefs
