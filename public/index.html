About

The intent of the Careers in Marketing document is to provide students with a perspective on how their major in marketing and degree in Business Administration can be applied to a future career setting. You will notice the marketing-related careers in this document have been categorized by three categories – analytical, creative and management. A lot of marketing roles cross over between the categories but reading through the specific Career Paths within this document on specific roles should help distinguish the differences and nuances among roles and determine what might be a good fit for you.

When deciding what role would be a good fit for you there are many factors to keep in mind. Here are a few thought starters to help get you thinking about what a good fit might be and determining what you want out of a career in Marketing.

What to Consider

Knowing what you dislike about a job or function is just as important as what you enjoy. There will most likely always be some things you don’t love about a job but having the majority of your job be something that you enjoy or are interested in can really help motivate you as an individual and benefit a company.

Curiosity throughout your career is a great thing to always be armed with. Even if you have been at a job for 10+ years, being curious about other industries, co-workers specific functions within an organization, competition, industry trends, etc. can always be beneficial to your work, growing your network and further developing your skill-set.

Strengths

What are your key strengths
as an individual?

Think about past jobs you may have had, projects you have been involved with at school, times you have worked in a group setting, your day-to-day life routine – What are you good at? What makes working through something (project, team dynamic, etc.) easier? What do you enjoy? What makes you feel accomplished? These qualities/characteristics can help you thrive in a job and finding a job that is a good fit to your strengths will help you feeling fulfilled day-to-day.
