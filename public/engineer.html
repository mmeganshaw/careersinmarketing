Development Engineer

A Day in the Life

The day starts by checking on data results from analysis tests that were likely submitted the day before. Some intensive analysis tests can take hours to complete, ranging from 1-24 hours. It is important for the engineers to be timely and plan ahead as to when they will submit these tests so that they have the necessary test results and data in time for deadlines, presentations and such. Data is collected and analyzed for trends against references and other subject models. It is now time to consult with the lead engineer for this project, which is similar to an advisor. Data is reviewed together and the lead engineer will provide feedback and direction on how they want the engineer(s) to proceed.

Next comes the meeting with the Project Team which includes engineers and associates from other groups including manufacturing, business sectors, and other design groups, and the Technical Project Leader leads this meeting. Updates from all functions are given and every team member has a good understanding of where the project stands. At this point, there is time to take the feedback received from the lead engineer and project team to adjust the design and run further iterative analyses to implement the changes and meet product requirement. Finally, to end the day, a stand-up informal ‘Work-In-Progress’ meeting is held with the Chief Engineer, who is the direct manager of the engineer and that function. This helps the chief engineer understand the project load on each individual engineer and how they are able to distribute work and projects in the future.
