Quality Assurance

A Day in the Life

Even though your work day is not 24 hours-a-day and 7 days-a-week, the manufacturing facility may well be, so your day starts with reviewing all out-of-process materials from the last day, which can be any produced part or assembly of parts that requires a disposition to get it back into the production flow due to its form or function not meeting an internal or external customerâ€™s specification.

Next you might investigate excessive variation and waste of your output product due to a certain machine or process by employing core quality tools like process capability monitoring and various problem-solving methodologies to maintain consistent and uniform production day after day.

Your ability to control and improve upon product quality is only as good as your ability to accurately measure your output product, so you will employ more core quality tools like Measurement System Analysis (MSA) or Gauge Reproducibility and Repeatability (Gauge R&R) to ensure your measurement system is sufficiently capable for the given specifications.

Another task at hand is to write a Work Instruction for a new job description or a Standard Operating Procedure for a new machine so that all operators of a specific process perform the operation the same way.

Unfortunately, everything does not go according to plan, so to respond to a previous quality spill, you will also have to work on a problem-solving investigation and report to be supplied to your customer (either internal or external) on what went wrong, what you did to mitigate the impact, and what you are doing and will continue to do to error-proof the system to ensure it never happens again.

To monitor and maintain the cross-functional operation of the facility, you will end your day interfacing with the various other departments (production, scheduling, finance, human resources, environmental and safety, maintenance and engineering, etc) to discuss current plant operations, financials, safety concerns, and any other factors impacting the success of the business. Finally, since you work in a continuous operation, you will complete your shift pass down to the oncoming quality team about active issues or upcoming things to monitor.
