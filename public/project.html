Project Management

A Day in the Life

The day starts with a daily standup meeting with the entire team to discuss daily progress over the last 24-hours and any new risks or issues across teams. Next, you might join a weekly all account meeting with leadership to discuss project status, burn summary and any high-level risks that need to be communicated to the client. Another task is identifying the follow-ups from morning meetings and taking action on next steps by sending a recap email and setting up any follow up meetings. Don’t forget in this morning’s standup meeting a lead team member mentioned the project timeline is delayed due to various reasons. With that said, you’ll need to validate that information with the applicable team members then discuss potential solutions to either get the project back on track or adjust the project timeline as needed. Keep in mind, if the project timeline is adjusted, it is pivotal to communicate that adjustment to the client with thoughtful rationale to keep open communication and transparency. Meanwhile, your inbox is overflowing with 46 unread emails from the team asking about permission issues on the new document repository. As the workday comes to a close you work to create a to-do list to help you prioritize efficiently the next day.

    • - Providing administrative support as needed across teams.
    • - Developing project strategies and communicating effectively across teams.
    • - Assessing project risks and providing solutions where applicable.
